Article 370: Planning & Execution

2. What Was Article 370?

The Special Agreement

  • ▶ Granted J&K its own constitution and flag.
  • ▶ Internal autonomy over most state matters.
  • Meant to be a temporary provision based on the Instrument of Accession.

Constitutional Limits

  • ▶ **Federal Subjects:** India controlled Defence, Foreign Affairs, and Communications.
  • ▶ **Consent:** Required J&K's Constituent Assembly consent for wider law application.
  • The Article defined the limits of the Union's power over the state.

3. The Gradual Build-Up to Removal

1

Erosion of Autonomy

Incremental application of central laws, gradually diluting J&K's special powers (1954-2019).

2

Political Mandate

Securing the necessary parliamentary majority and political will by 2019.

3

Legal Strategy

Formulating the Presidential Order (C.O. 272) for the internal deactivation of the Article.

4. The Coordinated Execution

01

Presidential Order (C.O. 272)

The key legal maneuver, reinterpreting the clause to bypass the now-defunct Constituent Assembly.

02

Parliamentary Legislation

The J&K Reorganisation Act was passed, formally revoking the Article and splitting the state.

Execution included the deployment of security forces and temporary communication restrictions to maintain law and order.

5. Arguments In Favour (Unity & Progress)

Integration & Equality

  • • Full Constitutional Unity: Realizes the goal of 'One Nation, One Constitution.'
  • • Equal Rights: Extends central laws protecting women's and marginalized groups' rights.

Economic & Security

  • • Investment Focus: Opens the region to external investment and faster economic development.
  • • Enhanced Security: Allows for direct Central government control and efficient security management.
Slide 5 / 8

6. Arguments Against (Democracy & Autonomy)

Procedural Legitimacy

  • ◼ Absence of Consent: Decision made without input from the J&K legislative body or citizens.
  • ◼ Subversion of Intent: Questionable use of the Article's own provisions for its permanent removal.

Erosion of Identity

  • ◼ Cultural Fear: Concerns over demographic change and loss of cultural autonomy and land protections.
  • ◼ Democratic Shutdown: Restrictions on communication and detention of political leaders.

7. Impacts & Implications

Political

Shift to UT status; increased central control over administration.

Legal Precedent

Supreme Court challenge set a major constitutional benchmark.

Economic

Opening of land and business investment to outsiders.

Social

Divided public reaction; fears of identity erosion vs. new opportunities.

8. Conclusion: A Defining Moment

The removal of Article 370 was a strategic and complex political move, marking the end of J&K's special status and initiating a new phase of national integration.

The Enduring Question:
How should a nation balance the dream of integration with the promise of historical autonomy?
